One Approach to Future Library Systems

One approach to trying to meet all of the demands for features and functionality in future library systems, is the ongoing work on what I'm calling a Catalog Pull Platform.

Three Sources of Library Systems Demand ("pull")

Originating in the Lean Startup movement for technology entrepreneurs that build upon the success Toyota and other manufacturing have had in implementing Lean Manufacturing ideas; the fundamental shift in thinking about library systems is away from trying to anticipate demand through "pushing" services and technology to our patrons and staff to instead identifying and responding to needs and demands for library technology by "pulling" directly from various constituencies in our libraries.

People

Institutions

Algorithms

Each of these pull sources provides us with a chance to ask what features and functionality are needed now and what features and functionality may be needed in the future. For each theme, we will ask:

  • Who are the people involved?
  • What are the outcomes our institutions want us to track?
  • How can we support the technology our patrons, vendors, business, and government through algorithmic access to our libraries collections and services?

The Minimum Viable Project (MVP) for the Catalog Pull Platform being actively developed through Build-Measure-Learn iterations is Colorado College's new catalog available at http://catalog.coloradocollege.edu/. This catalog uses a Aaron Schmidt design that he discusses in a blog posting, Catalog by Design
